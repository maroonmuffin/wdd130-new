Content

Home page

Why Wooden Toys? Research shows that children’s play that features natural elements like wood promotes concentration, focus, and a sense of calm more than other types of spaces. Additionally, touching the wood calms children down, which means it has a neurological impact on a child’s brain. Children naturally form bonds with toys when there are fewer to choose from. The open-ended nature of wooden toys allows children to use their imagination and spend more time with a single toy. These wooden toys are made from a natural material that connects children to nature. Wooden toys create a more nourishing and cozy feeling than plastic toys. Decorated with lead-free paint, and polished with beeswax wood polish, these are as eco-friendly as it gets. Benefits: The toy market is filled with flashing lights, loud colors, and lots of sounds. Features like these offer pleasure for young children, but they also tend to erase opportunities for problem solving and imagination. Wooden toys tend to be simpler, but they support cognitive milestones in ways that flashy toys can’t. For example, babies start to understand cause and effect: Hitting a toy on the floor makes a noise, and dropping one makes it disappear A wooden toy, like a building block set or a wooden puzzle, shows: “when I do something, it makes something else happen.” Because of the simplicity of wooden toys, parents can expect a tidy house instead of a playroom scattered with legos. They can be used in more ways than plastic toys, and they come with fewer pieces, so there is less mess.

Learning and Playing

Learning And Playing: Toys, in many ways, are an introduction to the physical world: they’re usually the first objects children touch, and play with. Wooden toys teach quieter lessons in cause and effect, creativity, and problem-solving than harsh plastic toys. These toys may seem very simple and plain, but they allow children to use their imagination and creativity. These basic wooden shapes seem like much more to children. They teach children to appreciate the simplicity and beauty around them. Wooden toys offer a beautiful, open-ended “blank canvas” for a child to use at their own pace. The extra buttons, sounds, and lights that can make other toys more instantly appealing tend to restrict and direct how children play with them. Wooden toys allow more ideas and fun with their simplicity.

Quality

Quality: Wooden toys never really go out of style. There’s rarely any big trend around the latest wooden pieces, but they’ve been enjoyed for generations and are still going strong. Unlike plastic digital trends that are ultimately forgotten in the heap of new trends, each year, wooden toys are as healthy as they are timeless. Wooden toys tend to be the ones most handed down and inherited. They’re not indestructible, but they are durable so long as you take proper care of them. Wood is among some of the safest materials nature provides for babies and children. These wooden toys are sanded and cleaned with beeswax to create a soft feel. Instead of harsh and sharp plastics, children can play with soft, smooth wooden figurines. These toys are made to last for years. They are durable and reliable, unlike plastic toys that eventually end up in the trash. The way toys look might not be on everyone’s list of criteria for selection, but beauty does matter—especially when choosing toys that will last. Whether painted, stained, or left natural, wooden toys, furniture, and art are more aesthetically appealing. Wooden toys are a natural fit for spaces in homes since they’re often stackable, easy to clean up, and attractive to display.
